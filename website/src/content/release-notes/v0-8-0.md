The operator terminal's detail panel had grown a stack of bordered sections — a cell-instruction card, flow progress, resources, the viewer — that pushed the drawing into a sliver and showed the same status, operation type, and routing in more than one place. This release gives it a single design philosophy: a calm header that tells an operator what they are holding, and everything else behind tabs that fill the screen. Instructions are flat and optional, the route lives in one place, and the 3D and PDF views load only when tapped.

The off-by-default location module is finished, too. When it is on, the terminal carries a Location tab and the completion picker both name the **next cell** a part is heading to and suggest a drop-off slot near it — so a finished part lands in the right lane and the next person finds it without hunting. The same panel works just as well on a phone as on a wall-mounted terminal.
